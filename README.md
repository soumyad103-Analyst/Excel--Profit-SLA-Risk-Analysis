# Excel--Profit-SLA-Risk-Analysis
Analyzed EV charging operations (92-day dataset) uncovering 36% SLA breaches and 14% revenue risk; identified downtime, discount erosion, and high-traffic cities as operational stress points.
# Business Problem
The after-sales division aimed to understand how rework jobs, warranty claims, and turnaround time (TAT) were affecting profitability and customer satisfaction.

# Dataset Overview
3,000 service jobs
Each row represents one service case
Key fields: Region, Issue Category, Severity, Service Type, Rework Flag, Warranty Claim, TAT Days, CSAT Score

# KPIs Designed

Total Revenue
Total Profit
Profit Margin %
Revenue at Risk
First Time Fix Rate
Repeat Visit Rate
Average CSAT
Analysis Approach
Compared rework vs first-time-fix performance
Evaluated TAT impact on customer satisfaction
Segmented revenue by severity and issue category
Calculated revenue linked to rework cost

# Key Insights
Rework contributes significantly to revenue leakage
73% customers reported low satisfaction
High-severity issues increase TAT and reduce CSAT
First Time Fix rate is relatively low

# Business Recommendations
Improve technician training to increase first-time fix
Reduce TAT for high-severity jobs
Monitor rework-related costs
